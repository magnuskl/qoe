This web application facilitates QoE studies of panoramic (and conventional) video. It is inteded for use with experiments that follow a full factorial design, meaning that more than one (usually two or three) independent variables assume different discreet values. The material being studied, that is, the panoramic video, is prepared in each possible combination of these values. Each variant is then compared to every other variant in a pairwise manner.

In this particular experiment, the video has been encoded in combinations of three different resolutions (480p, 720p, and 1080p) and two different CRF values (23 and 34). This yields 3 × 2 = 6 variants and 6 choose 2 = 15 pairs, noting that variants are compared only once and not with themselves. (Otherwise, there would have been 6 × 6 = 36 pairs.)

Just to keep track of the progress, the current pair and variant are displayed above the video. However, these numbers do not disclose the properties of each variant (i.e. encoding parameters), which is part of the purpose: it is supposed to be a double-blinded experiment, where neither the instructor nor the participant is biased by knowledge of the likely outcome. Pairs and variants appear in randomized sequence.

The controls are located below the video. Click the Previous and Next buttons to navigate between pairs. Click the Switch button (in the middle) to switch between variants within each pair. Hold and drag the video to change the viewing angle. Select the preferred variant by double-clicking the video or single-clicking the title, which may work better on handheld devices. A colored border will appear around the video's edge. Only one variant can be selected; selecting one will automatically deselect the other. All pairs must be assessed to complete the experiment.

In some cases the choice may be all but obvious, while in others it may not. There is no time limit, so take the time needed. There is also no correct or incorrect answer: QoE is a subjective measure. Click the Next button to start the experiment.
